# To-do Example
1 Conduct a comprehensive data quality audit.
A thorough audit builds the foundation for effective
pipeline management, forecasting, and reporting.
Identifying that 15% of opportunity records lack a
next-step date.
2 Analyze sales cycle efficiency and conversion rates.
Break down sales stages, measuring time-in-stage and
drop-off rates. Compare to industry benchmarks.
Discovering that deals linger in ‘Proposal Sent’ for
twice as long as the industry average.
3 Review territory and quota structures.
Check for balanced territory assignments to ensure fair
opportunity distribution.
Proposing territory re-alignment to better distribute
high-potential leads across the team.
4 Assess lead management and routing processes.
Trace the path of a new lead from entry (demo form,
event, etc.) to sales handoff—look for delays, misrouted
leads, or duplications.
Finding that leads from webinars are routed to a
dormant queue. Updating the rule to route them
directly to SDRs to cut off follow-up time.
5 Evaluate integration points between systems.
Streamlining integrations removes friction, saves
time/costs, and reduces data errors.
Creating a data-flow diagram showing how CRM,
marketing automation, CPQ, and BI tools connect.
Identifying data sync lags or manual workarounds.
# To-do Example
1 Develop metrics framework for GTM KPIs.
A clear framework ensures consistent data interpretation and
accountability for results. 
Align each metric (e.g., pipeline coverage, win
rate, NRR) with an owner (Sales, Marketing,
CS) and clarify definitions.
2 Create a roadmap for process improvements.
Prioritize initiatives by impact and complexity. Focus on 2–3
high-impact wins first to build momentum by tackling achievable
goals early, setting the stage for larger transformations later.
Ranking items: 1) automated lead routing, 2)
CPQ approval flow, 3) data governance
policy. Establishing timelines for each.
3 Define data governance standards.
Outline rules for data entry, validation, and ownership. Work with
enablement and top reps to improve buy-in and showcase how
clean data helps reps and managers win more deals.
Sales reps must update close date fields
weekly before deal reviews.
4 Develop a training plan for system users.
Identify gaps from your first 30 days (e.g., reps not fully using
the CRM). Develop training plans and create targeted sessions.
Conduct training on lead follow-up best
practices. Ideally in combination with internal
influencers like top reps and managers.
5 Identify technology gaps and optimization opportunities
Refer to your tool inventory to see if any platforms are
underutilized or duplicated.
You notice how your activity capture tool and
conversation intelligence solution log
activities. Consolidate to one.
# Deliverable
1 Data quality assessment report:
Summarize findings on data accuracy, completeness,
and consistency, with recommended fixes. Include
visuals to make the case clear to leadership.
2 Process improvement roadmap:
List improvement initiatives, rationale (why it
matters), estimated impact, timeline, and resource
needs. Show quick-win items first to demonstrate
early value, then outline medium/long-term projects.
3 Metrics framework document:
Clearly define each KPI and its formula, data source,
and owner (department or individual). Keep it in a
shared workspace so all teams reference it.
4 Technology optimization plan:
Outline optimizations to existing tools and proposed
new solutions. Incorporate cost-benefit analysis (e.g.,
ROI, increased adoption, reduced manual labor).

First 30 Days: Observe & Understand
Week 1-2: Information Garthering Week 3-4: Assessment & Quick Wins Key Deliverables
# To-do Example
1 Schedule 1:1s with all key stakeholders. 
Prepare a short list of questions about their biggest pain
points, success metrics, and current processes.
When meeting with the Head of Sales, focus on
understanding pipeline visibility gaps and areas
where reps lose deals.
2 Gain system access to all relevant platforms. 
Make a checklist of logins confirm you have the right user
permissions (admin vs. read-only). 
List relevant logins, e.g. CRM, Marketing
Automation, CPQ/Billing, BI tools, etc.
3 Audit current reporting and dashboards. 
Focus on high-level reports and document any
inconsistencies or data definition issues you spot.
E.g., pipeline forecast, lead / sales funnel, revenue
by product/segment. 
4 Understand the sales process & customer journey. Map
out each stage and note how leads flow between teams.
Gives you a baseline to measure improvements and
clarifies cross-functional dependencies.
Quick flowchart showing each handoff point;
highlight pain areas like lead response delays or
siloed customer data.
5 Review documentation on systems, processes, and
metrics. Ask for existing SOPs, training guides, or wikis. 
Sales might have existing ‘sales process’ docs.
# To-do Example
1 Identify and fix any immediate data quality issues.
Use a CRM health report to find duplicates, missing fields, etc.
If 20% of opportunities lack a close date,
propose and set up a simple validation rule.
2 Document current state of RevOps processes & systems.
Creates a baseline reference for future process redesigns.
Outline end-to-end workflows (lead acquisition → pipeline →
renewals). Note departmental handoff points and bottlenecks. 
Observe how a sales rep updates an
opportunity from creation to close; look for
manual data entry steps that could be
automated.
3 Create inventory of all tools in the tech stack.
Include each tool’s purpose, users, cost & integrations. Assess
usage frequency to spot overlapping/underutilized tools.
Discover that sales uses two different activity
capture solutions — decide if one can be
sunset to reduce costs.
4 Identify manual processes that could be automated.
Demonstrate tangible impact early on. Look for repeating tasks
(e.g., weekly CSV exports) that a workflow could handle. 
Use a simple automation rule to enrich lead
data instead of letting reps do it manually.
5 Deliver initial observations report to leadership.
Keep it concise: highlight major issues, quick wins, and long-
term opportunities. Use data to back your points.
Explain that pipeline forecasting accuracy is
off by ~15% due to inconsistent opportunity
stages; propose a simple data validation fix.
# Deliverable
1 Current state process map: Show each step of the
sales process from lead generation to renewal,
including who’s responsible and how data flows. 
2 Systems inventory document: List out every core
system, along with owners, access levels, major
integrations, and cost. Include usage stats.
3 Current customer journey map: Illustrate each
interaction. Note friction points (e.g., “website chat
aren’t routed to SDRs for up to 48 hours”)
4 Stakeholder interview summary: Summarize
priorities, pain points, and suggestions. Combine into
common themes to guide future RevOps projects.
5 Initial observations and recommendations report:
Outline findings, quick actions (e.g., duplicate record
cleanup), and longer-term initiatives. Include metrics.
Days 31-60: Analyze & Plan
Days 61-90: Implement & Optimize
Week 9-10: Implementation Week 11-12: Measurement & Refinement Key Deliverables
# To-do Example
1 Deploy data governance policies.
Refer back to the Data Governance Standards you
defined in the “Analyze & Plan” phase.
Enable mandatory fields and stage exit criteria in the
CRM. Assign data stewards per department to
enforce these policies.
2 Configure system enhancements.
Use insights from the technology optimization plan to
prioritize and execute system improvements.
Consolidate activity capture and conversation
intelligence into one tool to save tooling expenses
and improve data accuracy.
3 Implement “new/improved reporting.”
Use your metrics framework document as a reference for
consistent definitions and for building dashboards.
Launch a ‘Sales Cycle Efficiency’ dashboard showing
each stage’s average time and conversion rate over
time.
4 Launch training program for system users.
Ensures high adoption for newly configured tools and
workflows, preventing “change aversion”.
Run a CRM workshop highlighting automation rules
that turn previous admin time into selling time.
5 Execute high-priority process improvements.
Demonstrates tangible progress and reinforcing the trust
you built in earlier phases.
Complete territory realignment, ensuring each AE
now has a balanced book. Automate lead routing
rules so inbound leads skip manual triage.
# To-do Example
1 Measure impact of implemented changes.
Use the new dashboards and metrics you set up to compare
pre- and post-implementation performance
Did lead response time, sales cycle length,
etc. decrease?
2 Gather feedback from stakeholders.
Conduct short follow-up interviews or surveys with key leaders
(see stakeholder interview summary from first 30d).
Ask the Head of Sales if pipeline reviews feel
more accurate and whether reps are following
the updated data governance rules.
3 Refine processes based on initial results.
Identify quick course corrections, don’t wait for the next quarter.
If territory realignment reduced coverage in a
certain region, adjust again.
4 Document Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs).
Codify all new processes and system changes, referencing the
current state process map you created earlier. Update it to
reflect the improved workflows.
Create a short wiki or playbook for the new
CPQ approval process, including screenshots
and steps for reps.
5 Present results and long-term strategy to leadership
Highlight major wins (e.g., improved data accuracy, faster lead
response) and tie them to the RevOps strategy & roadmap.
Show a before/after comparison of key KPIs,
such as pipeline coverage or average sales
cycle length, to illustrate your impact.
# Deliverable
1 Impact assessment report:
Summarize KPI improvements and data quality gains.
Use visuals (charts, tables). Compare current metrics
to baseline values.
2 Updated dashboards and reports:
Make sure the new or revamped dashboards are live,
shared with relevant teams, and incorporate
consistent metric definitions from earlier.
3 RevOps strategy & roadmap document:
Incorporate learnings from the 90-day period, plus a
6–12 month outlook.
4 Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) document:
Centralize all new processes, policies, and tool usage
instructions. Store SOPs in a shared repository for
easy access.
