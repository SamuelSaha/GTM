## Page 31

Negotiating Your Success
● Doing your job well is not enough
● Find sponsors (champions) to advocate on your behalf
● Co-define what success looks like for your role with your manager
● Have it written down in your OKRs or better yet, create a Personal Development Plan
● Understand what the next level looks like (i.e. if you’re an Analyst, what does a Manager do that 
you don’t?)
● Perform at the Success level for your role and showcase greenshoots of the next level
● If you performed all of the above and don’t get promoted… it’s okay.
● You know what good looks like. You executed excellence. You negotiated it upfront.
● Doing this over and over throughout your career will lead to positive results

## Page 33

Signals it may be time to shake things up with your team
● Undefined career path or unwillingness internally to discuss defining a path
● High turnover in senior leadership (i.e. loss of sponsors, continuous success criteria reset)
● Low or no growth
● You were up for promotion and a leader was hired above you
● Severe external factor negatively impacting your industry
● The company has outgrown your skillset and you want to continue doing more of the same
● Diminishing marginal learning and growth opportunities available

## Page 34

Take stock of all you’ve achieved
● Start with the company’s OKRs
● List out all of the strategic initiatives you were a part of and all intiatives you led
● Think through the S.T.A.R. format
○ Situation: prior to this initiative, sales cycles were 90 days
○ Task: develop a Discovery and Demo process which reduces the current cycle of 30 days 
down to 15 days
○ Action: 1) develop a Qualification standard, 2) develop a seamless handoff process 
between SDR and AE, 3) develop a Discovery process which helps to increase the 
personalization of the demo
○ Result: sales cycles reduced by 14 days, 10 of which were in the Discovery stage. Although 
we did not cut from 30 to 15, we did achieve significant results by shrinking our Discovery 
from 30 days to 20.
● Next Steps: we’re already working on whether we can shrink this stage any further
● Impact: Reducing the sales cycle of a deal increases the average deal velocity
